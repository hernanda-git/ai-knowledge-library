## 4. Emerging Paradigms

### 4.1 Ambient Intelligence as Infrastructure

The future isn't individual wearable devices — it's ambient intelligence woven into the infrastructure itself:

```
2026: Device-Centric
├── User wears smart glasses
├── User wears AI earbuds
├── User carries smartphone
└── Each device has its own AI

2028: Hybrid
├── Wearables + Smart home
├── Devices share context
├── Home provides ambient sensing
└── AI spans device boundaries

2030: Infrastructure-Centric
├── Buildings have embedded AI
├── Surfaces are interactive
├── Wearables are optional (thin layer)
└── Intelligence is environmental

2032+: Invisible Computing
├── No visible devices
├── AI is everywhere, nowhere
├── Interaction is natural/seamless
```

When many AI wearables coexist in a space, emergent intelligence arises:

```python

    """
    When multiple AI wearables share (anonymized) context,
    the space itself becomes intelligent.
    """
    
    def __init__(self):
        self.local_devices = DeviceMesh()
        self.shared_context = SharedContextStore()
        self.federated_model = FederatedLearningModel()
    
    def aggregate_anonymous_context(self):
        """
        Aggregate context from all devices in the space
        without compromising individual privacy.

            "occupancy": self.local_devices.count_anonymous(),
            "activity_distribution": self.local_devices.get_activity_distribution(),

            "temperature_preference": self.local_devices.get_temperature_consensus(),
            "lighting_preference": self.local_devices.get_lighting_consensus()

        Use collective intelligence to optimize the environment.
```

## 5. Ethical Considerations

### 5.1 Key Ethical Challenges

| Challenge | Description | Mitigation |
|-----------|-------------|------------|
| **Surveillance creep** | Wearables enable mass surveillance | Legal limits, hardware kill switches |
| **Consent erosion** | Bystanders can't consent to recording | Face blurring, audio redaction |
| **Algorithmic bias** | AI performs differently for different demographics | Diverse training data, bias testing |
| **Digital divide** | AI wearables are expensive | Subsidized programs, open-source alternatives |
| **Attention economy** | Always-on AI can be addictive | Usage limits, digital wellness features |
| **Autonomy** | AI making decisions for humans | Human override, transparency |
| **Employer surveillance** | Workplace wearables monitoring workers | Clear policies, worker protections |
