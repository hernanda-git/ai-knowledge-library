# 01 — AI Business Models Playbooks: Overview

## What This Playbook Collection Covers

This comprehensive playbook collection is a strategic resource for founders, product managers, investors, and operators building AI-native businesses. It covers the full spectrum of monetization, go-to-market, and operational models for AI companies in 2025–2026.

The AI industry has matured beyond simple API wrappers. Today's AI businesses span SaaS platforms, agentic services, consulting practices, fine-tuning studios, marketplace models, and hybrid approaches. This collection provides actionable templates, real-world frameworks, and data-driven guidance for each model.

### Why These Playbooks Exist

The AI business model landscape is evolving rapidly. In 2024–2025, we saw:

- **Pricing volatility**: OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google have cut API prices 5–10x in 18 months
- **New categories emerge**: Agent-as-a-Service, outcome-based pricing, AI consulting pods
- **Enterprise adoption accelerating**: 72% of enterprises now have an AI budget line item (Gartner 2025)
- **PLG + Sales-Led hybrid becoming standard**: Most successful AI companies use both motions
- **Open-source disruption**: Llama, Mistral, and others compress margins on commoditized models

These playbooks are designed to help you navigate this landscape and build a durable AI business.

## Key Themes Across Playbooks

### 1. Hybrid Monetization Is the New Normal

Most successful AI companies in 2025 use 2–3 revenue models simultaneously:

- **Usage-based API + Subscription**: e.g., OpenAI offers both API (usage) and ChatGPT Plus (subscription)
- **Services + Software**: Anthropic offers consulting alongside Claude API
- **Freemium + Enterprise**: Most AI SaaS products (Notion AI, Grammarly, GitHub Copilot)

### 2. Value-Based Pricing Is Essential

Commodity pricing (per-token, per-seat) works for infrastructure but leaves money on the table. The most successful AI companies price based on value delivered:

- **Outcome pricing**: Charge per task completed, not per API call
- **ROI-based**: Price at 10–20% of the value created for the customer
- **Tiered by capability**: Basic vs. pro vs. enterprise tiers with widening feature gaps

### 3. Unit Economics Matter More Than Ever

AI business models have unique cost structures:

| Cost Component | Typical Range | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Inference compute | 20–40% of COGS | Dropping 30–50% YoY |
| Training (once) | $100K–$100M | Amortized over customer base |
| R&D / Fine-tuning | 15–25% of revenue | Ongoing for model improvement |
| Customer acquisition | 30–80% of first-year revenue | Varies by channel |
| Cloud infrastructure | 10–20% of revenue | Hosting, vector DBs, etc. |

### 4. The AI Adoption Maturity Curve

Different business models map to different stages of AI maturity:

| Stage | Business Model | Typical Pricing | Key Metric |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Experimental** | Free tier, API credits | Freemium | Activation rate |
| **Tactical** | SaaS per-seat, usage | $10–$100/user/mo | Time-to-value |
| **Strategic** | Enterprise, custom | $50K–$1M+/yr | ROI delivered |
| **Transformational** | Outcome-based, agentic | % of value | Revenue per agent |

### Key Trends Driving Business Model Innovation

1. **Agentic Workflows**: Moving from copilot to autonomous agents changes pricing from per-seat to per-outcome
2. **Model Commoditization**: Open-source models compress margins on basic inference; value shifts to applications and fine-tuning
3. **Vertical AI**: Specialized models for legal, medical, financial services command 3–5x pricing vs. horizontal
4. **Regulation**: EU AI Act, US Executive Orders create compliance needs that open new service opportunities
5. **AI-Native Companies**: Companies built entirely on AI (no human-in-the-loop) scale differently and have different unit economics

## Metrics Glossary

| Term | Definition | Benchmark |
| --- | --- | --- |
| ARR | Annual Recurring Revenue | Target: >$1M seed, >$10M Series A |
| MRR | Monthly Recurring Revenue | Target: >$80K seed |
| NRR | Net Revenue Retention | Median AI SaaS: 115% |
| GRR | Gross Revenue Retention | Target: >90% |
| LTV | Lifetime Value | AI median: 24–36 months |
| CAC | Customer Acquisition Cost | PLG: $500–$2K; Enterprise: $10K–$50K |
| LTV/CAC | Ratio | Healthy: >3x, Great: >5x |
| Magic Number | (Q Revenue - Q-1 Revenue) × 4 / Q-1 S&M Spend | >0.75 great |
| Payback Period | CAC / (MRR × Gross Margin) | <12 months ideal |
| Gross Margin | (Revenue - COGS) / Revenue | AI SaaS target: >70% |
| Net Burn | Cash out - Cash in per month | Should decrease as % of revenue |
| Runway | Cash / Net Burn | >18 months ideal |
