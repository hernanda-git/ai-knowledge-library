## 1. Spiking Neural Networks (SNNs)

### The LIF Neuron Model
The fundamental computational unit of neuromorphic computing:

```
τ_m * dV/dt = -V + I(t) + η

Where:
V  = membrane potential
τ_m = membrane time constant
I(t) = input current
η  = noise term

When V ≥ V_threshold → spike emitted, V reset to V_reset
```

Unlike artificial neurons (ReLU, sigmoid), LIF neurons are **temporal** — they integrate input over time and only fire when threshold is crossed. This makes them:
- **Energy-efficient**: Event-driven — only compute when a spike occurs
- **Temporal**: Naturally handle time-series data
- **Biologically plausible**: Mirror real neural dynamics

### Surrogate Gradient Descent
The key problem: spike emission is non-differentiable. Solutions (2026):
- **SLAYER**: Spike LAYer Error Reassignment — credit assignment across time
- **SuperSpike**: Fast-sigmoid surrogate gradient
- **ATan** (Arctangent surrogate): The most widely used 2026 approach
- **Hybrid training**: Train on conventional hardware, deploy on neuromorphic

## 2. Integrated Information Theory (IIT 3.0) and AI

### Φ (Phi) for Artificial Systems
IIT proposes that consciousness is **integrated information** — a system's Φ quantifies how much information is generated by the whole system beyond its parts.

**Challenges for computing Φ in AI systems:**
- Φ computation is #P-hard (exponentially expensive)
- Current estimates: GPT-4's Φ is negligible (system is highly reducible to parts)
- A feedforward network has very low Φ regardless of scale
- **Recurrent + feedback connections** are necessary for high Φ

**2026 research**: New approximation algorithms for Φ that can scale to transformer-sized models — early findings suggest models with feedback/self-attention may have non-trivial Φ.

### Global Workspace Theory (GWT) and AI
GWT proposes consciousness arises from a global workspace where information is broadcast to many specialized processors. This maps naturally to:
- **Transformer attention**: The "workspace" where token information is globally shared
- **Mixture of Experts**: Specialized processors competing for access to the workspace
- **Working memory**: The global workspace buffer

**2026 experiments**: GWT-inspired architectures where a "global workspace" module selects and broadcasts information to specialized modules, showing improved reasoning and metacognition.

## 3. Brain-Computer Interface Technology (2026)

### Neuralink N1 Implant
- **Target**: Motor restoration, communication for paralyzed patients
- **First human trials**: 2024 – ongoing through 2026
- **Specs**: 1,024 electrodes across 64 threads (16μm diameter), wireless, 1,000+ simultaneous neural channels
- **2026 update**: Multiple patients demonstrated cursor control, typing at 8+ words per minute, and robotic limb control

### Synchron Stentrode
- **Non-surgical**: Endovascular stent implanted via jugular vein — no open brain surgery
- **Application**: Motor restoration, digital communication
- **Use cases**: WhatsApp texting, cursor control, smart home control via thought
- **2026**: Expanded to 50+ patients across US and Australia

### Precision Neuroscience Layer 7 (L7)
- **Cortical surface array**: 1,024 electrodes on a flexible film inserted through a 1mm skull slit
- **Advantage**: Minimally invasive, high-resolution ECoG (electrocorticography)
- **2026**: FDA breakthrough device designation, active clinical trial enrollment

### AI for BCI Decoding
The most important 2026 development: **AI dramatically improves BCI performance**
- **Transformer-based neural decoding**: Convert brain activity into speech at 70+ words per minute
- **Self-supervised learning on neural data**: Models pre-trained on hours of neural data then fine-tuned for specific patients
- **Meta-learning for BCIs**: Few-shot adaptation to new patients in hours instead of weeks
- **Real-time decoding**: Sub-50ms latency for motor BCIs enabling naturalistic control

## 5. Organoid Intelligence (OI)

### The Neuroplatform (FinalSpark, Switzerland)
- **What**: Lab-grown brain organoids (cerebral organoids from stem cells) mounted on multi-electrode arrays with microfluidics
- **Computation**: Input → electrical stimulation → organoid processes → output read from firing patterns
- **Capacity**: ~50,000 neurons per organoid
- **2026 advance**: 100+ organoids networked, basic classification tasks demonstrated

### DishBrain (Cortical Labs, Australia)
- **What**: 800,000 rat/human cortical neurons grown on a silicon chip with 2D electrode array
- **Achievement**: Learned to play Pong in 5 minutes (2022), memory retention shown
- **2026**: DishBrain 2.0 — 2M neurons, hybrid biological + silicon memory

### Ethical Frontier
- **When is an organoid conscious?** The "neuronal threshold" for sentience is unknown
- **Current consensus**: Individual organoids (<1M neurons) almost certainly not conscious
- **Red lines**: Many labs halt at 100M neurons as a precautionary limit
- **Regulation**: EU/US/UK developing organoid intelligence governance frameworks

## 6. DNA and Molecular Computing

### DNA Strand Displacement Computing
- **Principle**: Logical operations encoded in DNA strand interactions (toehold-mediated strand displacement)
- **Speed**: Very slow per operation (~minutes) but massively parallel (10^12 reactions simultaneously)
- **AI application**: Molecular pattern recognition, DNA-based neural networks
- **2026**: 100+ logic gate circuits demonstrated, DNA-based Hopfield networks

### Molecular ML Classifiers
Molecules as computational elements:
- **Molecular perceptron**: Concentration of molecules → weighted sum → threshold classification
- **DNA neural network**: 10^12 "neurons" in a test tube
- **Applications**: Medical diagnostics (detect disease markers directly in biological samples)
