## 1. Introduction

AI's impact varies dramatically across industries. Some sectors are experiencing rapid transformation with significant job displacement, while others are seeing AI augment human capabilities without reducing headcount. This document provides a detailed, sector-by-sector analysis of AI's workforce impact, including specific roles affected, timeline for changes, and actionable guidance for workers in each industry.

### Methodology

Each industry analysis includes:
- **Net Impact Assessment**: Overall effect on employment
- **Roles Created**: New job categories specific to the industry
- **Roles Displaced**: Jobs at highest risk of automation
- **Timeline**: When changes are expected to occur
- **Preparation Strategies**: What workers can do to adapt

## 2. Technology Sector

### 2.1 Overview

**Net Impact**: Strongly Positive
**Employment Change**: +15% by 2028
**Key Dynamic**: AI creates more tech jobs than it destroys

The technology sector is both the creator and primary beneficiary of AI. While AI automates certain coding and testing tasks, it simultaneously creates massive demand for AI-related roles.

### 2.2 Roles Created

| Role | Growth Rate | Avg. Salary | Key Skills |
|------|------------|-------------|------------|
| AI/ML Engineer | +120% | $165K-$220K | ML frameworks, model training |
| AI Infrastructure Engineer | +95% | $155K-$210K | Cloud, Kubernetes, ML ops |
| AI Product Manager | +85% | $150K-$200K | AI strategy, product design |
| AI Safety Engineer | +200% | $145K-$200K | AI safety, alignment |
| AI Evaluator | +190% | $135K-$185K | Testing, benchmarking |
| Prompt Engineer | +200% | $120K-$170K | Prompt design, LLMs |
| AI Developer Relations | +110% | $140K-$190K | Technical communication |

### 2.3 Roles Displaced

| Role | Decline Rate | Timeline | Replacement |
|------|-------------|----------|-------------|
| Junior Developer (routine tasks) | -25% | 2025-2028 | AI coding assistants |
| QA Tester (manual) | -40% | 2025-2027 | AI testing tools |
| Technical Writer (basic docs) | -30% | 2025-2028 | AI documentation tools |
| DevOps (routine tasks) | -20% | 2026-2029 | AI automation |

### 2.4 Key Dynamics

1. **AI Augments, Not Replaces**: Senior developers become 2-3x more productive with AI
2. **New Specializations Emerge**: AI safety, evaluation, and orchestration are new fields
3. **Full-Stack Becomes AI-Stack**: Developers need AI/ML knowledge alongside traditional skills
4. **Open Source Accelerates**: AI tools democratize development capabilities

### 2.5 Preparation Strategies

- **For Current Developers**: Learn AI/ML fundamentals, master AI coding tools
- **For New Entrants**: Focus on AI-native development from the start
- **For Managers**: Invest in team AI skills, restructure for AI-augmented workflows

## 3. Financial Services

### 3.1 Overview

**Net Impact**: Moderately Negative
**Employment Change**: -8% by 2028
**Key Dynamic**: Significant automation of analysis and processing roles

Financial services is heavily adopting AI for analysis, trading, compliance, and customer service. While some roles are being automated, others (especially those requiring judgment and client relationships) are being augmented.

### 3.2 Roles Created

| Role | Growth Rate | Avg. Salary | Key Skills |
|------|------------|-------------|------------|
| AI Risk Analyst | +65% | $130K-$180K | Risk modeling, AI tools |
| AI Compliance Officer | +80% | $120K-$165K | Regulatory knowledge, AI governance |
| AI Fraud Detection Specialist | +90% | $135K-$185K | ML, anomaly detection |
| Quantitative AI Researcher | +70% | $160K-$250K | Statistics, ML, finance |
| AI Customer Experience Manager | +55% | $110K-$150K | AI tools, customer success |

### 3.3 Roles Displaced

| Role | Decline Rate | Timeline | Replacement |
|------|-------------|----------|-------------|
| Trading Analyst (routine) | -45% | 2025-2028 | Algorithmic trading |
| Data Entry/Processing | -60% | 2024-2027 | AI automation |
| Basic Financial Analysis | -35% | 2026-2029 | AI analytics |
| Compliance Monitoring | -25% | 2026-2029 | AI compliance tools |

### 3.4 Key Dynamics

1. **Quantitative Roles Decline**: Routine analysis increasingly automated
2. **Advisory Roles Augment**: Human advisors become more valuable with AI support
3. **Compliance Transforms**: AI creates new compliance needs while automating routine checks
4. **Customer Service Shifts**: AI handles routine queries, humans handle complex issues

### 3.5 Preparation Strategies

- **For Analysts**: Develop AI tools expertise, focus on judgment-intensive work
- **For Advisors**: Use AI to enhance client relationships, not replace them
- **For Compliance**: Learn AI governance, become AI compliance specialists

## 4. Healthcare

### 4.1 Overview

**Net Impact**: Neutral to Positive
**Employment Change**: +5% by 2028
**Key Dynamic**: AI augments clinical work, automates administrative tasks

Healthcare is unique in that AI enhances rather than replaces most clinical roles. The physical nature of patient care and the high stakes of medical decisions mean human oversight remains essential.

| AI Health Data Engineer | +90% | $125K-$175K | Health data, interoperability |

### 4.3 Roles Displaced

|------|-------------|----------|-------------|
| Medical Coding | -50% | 2025-2028 | AI coding tools |

| Administrative Scheduling | -40% | 2025-2028 | AI scheduling tools |

### 4.4 Key Dynamics

1. **Clinical Roles Augment**: AI assists diagnosis, doesn't replace clinicians
2. **Administrative Roles Automate**: Significant reduction in paperwork and admin
3. **New AI-Clinical Roles**: Hybrid roles combining medical and AI expertise
4. **Patient Interaction Remains Human**: Empathy and communication stay human

### 4.5 Preparation Strategies

- **For Clinicians**: Learn to work with AI diagnostic tools
- **For Admin Staff**: Transition to AI-resistant roles or develop AI skills
- **For Researchers**: Embrace computational biology and AI drug discovery

## 14. Cross-Industry Patterns

### 14.1 Universal Trends

| Pattern | Description | Industries Most Affected |
|---------|-------------|------------------------|
| **Admin Automation** | AI automates paperwork and data entry | All industries |
| **Customer Service Transformation** | AI handles routine queries | Retail, Finance, Healthcare |
| **Analysis Augmentation** | AI enhances human analysis | Finance, Healthcare, Government |
| **Content Generation** | AI creates basic content | Media, Marketing, Education |
| **Predictive Maintenance** | AI prevents equipment failures | Manufacturing, Energy, Transportation |
| **Quality Control** | AI inspects and tests | Manufacturing, Healthcare |

### 14.2 Industry Vulnerability Matrix

| Industry | Admin Roles | Technical Roles | Creative Roles | Client-Facing Roles |
|----------|------------|----------------|---------------|-------------------|
| Technology | High | Low (augment) | Low | Low |
| Finance | High | Medium | Low | Low |
| Healthcare | High | Low (augment) | Low | Low |
| Manufacturing | High | Medium | Low | N/A |
| Retail | High | Medium | Low | Medium |
| Education | High | Low | Low | Low |
| Legal | High | Medium | Low | Low |
| Media | Medium | Medium | High | Low |

### 14.3 The Resilience Hierarchy

Across all industries, job resilience follows this hierarchy:

**Most Resilient (Hardest to Automate):**
1. Complex physical manipulation in unstructured environments
2. Creative vision and ideation
3. Deep relationship building and negotiation
4. Ethical judgment and moral reasoning
5. Leadership and team motivation
6. Cross-domain synthesis and strategic thinking

**Least Resilient (Easiest to Automate):**
1. Repetitive data processing
2. Standard report generation
3. Routine decision-making based on clear rules
4. Pattern matching and classification
5. Basic content creation from templates
6. Standard communication and documentation

### 14.4 Investment Priority by Industry

| Industry | AI Investment Priority | Workforce Investment Priority |
|----------|----------------------|------------------------------|
| Technology | Build AI products | AI skills training |
| Finance | AI for analysis and compliance | Reskilling analysts |
| Healthcare | AI for diagnosis and admin | Clinical AI training |
| Manufacturing | AI for quality and maintenance | Technical upskilling |
| Retail | AI for personalization | Customer experience skills |
| Education | AI for personalization | Teacher AI training |
| Legal | AI for research and review | Legal tech skills |
